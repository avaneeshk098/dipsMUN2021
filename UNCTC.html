UN Counter Terrorism Committee (UN CTC)

The Western Horn of Africa has never been able to rid its shackles which draw a nation back post colonialism, as after the era of the European Rulers in this region, their growth and development has incessantly been deterred by intercommunal and extremist and terrorist acts, leaving West Africa scarred and shaken.

Brimming with problems not limited to violent attacks on civilians by violent organizations,Human rights violations from terrorists as well as the Security Forces, child recruitment for forced fighting has left the land in an extremely volatile state. The lack of political stability and development, poverty, hunger and malnourishment leads to a perfect ground for breeding of terror. Making their stand in the region stronger lies them holding the lawless desert of SAHEL as their safe haven. The recent attacks taking the lives of 137 in Niger and 160 in Burkina Faso coupled with the rise of Boko Haram and The ISGS among other Terror groups have strongly indicated a need for a joint, strong and comprehensive action, led by the Local countries along with the foreign players with a keen interest in the region.

The Committee rests its responsibilities with the delegates, to come together to strengthen the region and make the prospect of peace not a fantasy in the region.
