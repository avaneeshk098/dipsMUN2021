La Cosa Nostra (Semi-crisis)

Behind every successful fortune there is a crime. It's the turn of a new decade and the dawn of the darkest days of the US economy - 1930. Alongside the murky waters of the New York harbour, crime is the economy and the law is an encumbrance destined to be manipulated. With the Prohibition era at its peak and the Great Depression ravaging through the money supply of the nation, unprecedented challenges await the Italian mafioso families of NYC. The romanticised pinstripe suit era and household popularity of the mafioso names concealed an intense war plagued by a labyrinth of ethnic, generational and inter-mob conflicts that morphed the previously monotone organised crime operations into a multidimensional syndicate on the verge of mutually assured destruction.

The two warring factions of the Italian mafia, led by Giuseppe “Joe” Masseria and Salvatore Maranzano respectively, have a seething enmity. While Masseria consolidated power in the US, Maranzano, originating from Castellammare del Golfo, rose through the ranks of the Sicilian mafia (dubbed the Castallammarese clan/family) by expanding operations in the US. These two clans are going head to head for sole control of the New York mafia operations. Under the guise of the ostensible purpose of financial gain, the passions of ethnicity and differences in business ideologies are simmering away. This concoction of lust for power, money and control has boiled over on the eve of the murder of Gaetano Reina on February 26, 1930.

The first stone has been cast. Family loyalty is becoming conditional and people are beginning to defect. Unbeknownst to the bosses who are trading blows, yet another tangent of conflict is being envisaged on generational differences. There are no permanent friends or foes, only permanent interests. In the words of Mario Puzo, “It's dangerous to be an honest man.” Trust is the currency in 1930 and just like the US dollar, it is in a deficit. Welcome to the manifestation of the metaphor "Chaos is a ladder" - the quagmire of La Cosa Nostra.
