Message from the secretary general

Dear Delegates,

“Years ago anthropologist Margret Mead was asked by a student what she thought was the first sign of civilisation in a culture. The student expected Mead to talk about fishhooks or clay pots or grinding stones. But no. Mean said the first sign of civilisation in a culture was a femur that had been broken and then healed.
“Mead explained that in the animal kingdom, if you break your leg, you die. A broken femur that is healed is evidence that someone has taken time to stay with the one who fell, has bound up the wound, has carried the person to safety and has tended the person through recovery. Helping someone through difficulty is where civilisation starts.”
Much later YouTuber John Green discovered that, “It’s all a lie.” We don’t know the source of this quote, the start of civilisation has more to do with taxes than any of us would like, and a broken femur doesn’t automatically mean death in the animal kingdom. But worst of all, people have died from femur fractures while living in civilisation. Like John says, “It’s a seductive lie, perhaps because at its core its true.”
For us as a civilisation, these are not unprecedented times. We have faced pandemics before, but more than that we have faced poverty, conflict, warfare, discrimination and tyrants. All conditions that put strain on global structures, grow exponentially and kill with just as much discrimination toward the already disadvantaged.
In this DiPSMUN, I hope all of you delegates advocate not only for yourselves but for each other as well.
On behalf of my alma mater Delhi Public School, Bangalore- South, it is my pleasure to invite you all to the ninth edition on DiPSMUN!

Yours,
Rutuja Uttarwar.

P.S. We realise that an online edition of a MUN can cause several problems, the entire Executive Board, and especially our organisers and teachers will strive to make this an experience of fruitful discussion, and conversation between your peers. Please feel free to reach out to me!
